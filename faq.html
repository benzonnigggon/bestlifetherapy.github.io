Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ'S)

What is the difference between Counselling and Coaching?

Counselling focuses on specific problems in hand and look into emotional resolutions to past problems to move forwards where “healing” is one of the main objectives. Counselling is more process driven and even though the work will be to focus on “here and now”, the counsellor and client will also explore past issues to identify what is holding them back.

Coaching focuses on creating a new life path to achieve certain goals. It is more about action, performance and progress driven.

What about the Stigma attached to seeing a Counsellor?

Seeing a counsellor is not a sign of weakness but a strength. It takes special courage to seek help when you are in need. Many people feel the need to see a counsellor at some stage in their lives but the stigma of seeing a counsellor holds them back. The therapy or coaching can be one of the most wonderful experience and a positive step to taking back control of your life when you feel lost, stuck and confused.

How often do I need Counselling?

It really depends on you. Some clients will only require a few sessions before they see a benefit. There is no set time limit to counselling. I have had some clients who only need one session. What’s important is your health and well-being and we will figure out along the way how frequent sessions you will require.

How long will each session be?

Sessions run for 50 minutes.

Do I need a referral from my Doctor or a Mental Health Plan?

You do not need a referral from your Doctor or a Mental Health Plan to come and see a counsellor. A Mental Health Plan is only required if you are going to see a Clinical Psychologist.
